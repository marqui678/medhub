 Systemic means affecting the entire body, rather than a single organ or body part. For example, systemic disorders, such as  high blood pressure , or systemic diseases, such as  influenza , affect the entire body. An infection that is in the bloodstream is called a systemic infection.  An infection that affects only one body part or organ is called a localized infection. Read More
