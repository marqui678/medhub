
 Sebaceous adenoma

 A sebaceous adenoma is a noncancerous tumor of an oil-producing gland in the skin. Information 
 
 A sebaceous adenoma is a small bump. There is usually only one, and it is usually found on the face, scalp, belly, back, or chest. It may be a sign of a serious internal disease. If you have several small bumps of the sebaceous glands, this is called sebaceous hyperplasia. Such bumps are usually are harmless and often found on the face. They are not a sign of serious disease. They are more common with age. They may be treated if you do not like how they look. Alternative Names 
 
 Sebaceous hyperplasia; Hyperplasia - sebaceous; Adenoma - sebaceous Images
