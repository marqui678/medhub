
 Post-splenectomy syndrome 
 
 
 
 To use the sharing features on this page, please enable JavaScript. 
 Post-splenectomy syndrome can occur after surgery to remove the spleen. It consists of a group of symptoms and signs such as:  Destruction of red blood cells Increased risk of severe infections, from bacteria such as  Streptococcus pneumoniae  and  Neisseria meningitidis Thrombocytosis (increased  platelet count , which can cause  blood clots )  Possible Complications 
 
 Possible long-term medical problems include: Hardening of the arteries ( atherosclerosis ) Pulmonary hypertension  (a disease affecting the blood vessels in your lungs)  Alternative Names
