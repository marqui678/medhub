
 Kinins 
 
 
 
 To use the sharing features on this page, please enable JavaScript. 
 Kinins are proteins in the blood that cause inflammation and affect blood pressure (especially low blood pressure). They also: Increase blood flow throughout the body Make it easier for fluids to pass through small blood vessels Stimulate pain receptors Are part of a complex system that helps repair damaged tissue in the body  References
