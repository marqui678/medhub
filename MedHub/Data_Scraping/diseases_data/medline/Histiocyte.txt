
 Histiocyte 
 
 
 
 To use the sharing features on this page, please enable JavaScript. 
 A histiocyte is a type of immune cell. It destroys foreign substances to protect the body from infection. Information 
 
 Histiocytes do not travel through the blood. Instead, they remain in one part of the body. Histiocytes are found in many organs and tissues, including the: Brain Breast tissue Liver Lung Lymph nodes Placenta Spleen Tonsils An abnormal number of histiocytes leads to a disease called Langerhans cell histiocytosis (previously called  histiocytosis X ).
