
 Hypersplenism 
 
 
 
 To use the sharing features on this page, please enable JavaScript. 
 Hypersplenism is an overactive spleen. The spleen is an organ found in the upper left side of your abdomen. The spleen helps filter old and damaged cells from your bloodstream. If your spleen is overactive, it removes the blood cells too early and too quickly. The spleen plays a key role in helping your body fight infections. Problems with the spleen can make you more likely to develop infections. Causes 
 
 Common causes of hypersplenism include: Cirrhosis  (advanced liver disease) Lymphoma Malaria Tuberculosis Various connective tissue and inflammatory diseases  Symptoms 
 
 Symptoms include:  Enlarged spleen Low level of one or more types of blood cells Feeling full too soon after eating Stomach pain on the left side  Images
