
 Exfoliative dermatitis 
 
 
 
 To use the sharing features on this page, please enable JavaScript. 
 Exfoliative dermatitis is widespread  scaling , peeling, and flaking of the skin. It is often accompanied by, itching, skin redness (erythroderma), and hair loss.  Causes 
 
 This condition may occur in severe cases of many common skin conditions, such as eczema and psoriasis. It is sometimes caused by an allergy to drugs or another body-wide disease.  Treatment 
 
 A person with severe symptoms often needs hospital care, at times in an intensive-care burn unit. This is needed in order to monitor fluid loss through the skin.  Alternative Names 
 
 Erythroderma; Dermatitis exfoliativa; Pruritus - exfoliative dermatitis Images
