
 Anaerobic bacteria 
 
 
 
 To use the sharing features on this page, please enable JavaScript. 
 Anaerobic bacteria are bacteria that do not live or grow when oxygen is present. In humans, these bacteria are most commonly found in the gastrointestinal tract. They play a role in conditions such as  appendicitis ,  diverticulitis , and  perforation of the bowel . Alternative Names
