
 Flat bones 
 
 
 
 To use the sharing features on this page, please enable JavaScript. 
 Flat bones are made up of a layer of spongy bone between two thin layers of compact bone. They have a flat shape, not rounded. Examples include the skull and rib bones. Flat bones have marrow, but they do not have a bone marrow cavity. References
