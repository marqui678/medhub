
 Liquid nitrogen 
 
 
 
 To use the sharing features on this page, please enable JavaScript. 
 Liquid nitrogen is a chemical that is super cold, about -328°F (-200°C). Liquid nitrogen will instantly freeze anything it touches. It is used to kill cells that make up diseased or cancerous tissue. Tissue that has been frozen dries out and falls off. References 
 
 Habif TP. Dermatologic surgical procedures. In: Habif TP, ed.  Clinical Dermatology . 5th ed. Philadelphia, PA: Elsevier Mosby; 2009:chap 27.
