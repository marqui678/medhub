
 Gross motor control

 Gross motor control is the ability to make large, general movements (such as waving an arm or lifting a leg). It requires proper coordination and function of muscle, bones, and nerves. Information 
 
 Gross motor control is a milestone in the development of an infant. Infants develop gross motor control before they develop the ability to make small, precise movements known as  fine motor control .  References
