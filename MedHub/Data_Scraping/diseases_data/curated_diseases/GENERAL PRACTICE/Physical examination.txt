
 Physical examination

 During a physical examination, a health care provider studies your body to determine if you do or do not have a physical problem. A physical examination usually includes: Inspection  (looking at the body) Palpation  (feeling the body with fingers or hands) Auscultation  (listening to sounds) Percussion  (producing sounds, usually by tapping on specific areas of the body)  References
