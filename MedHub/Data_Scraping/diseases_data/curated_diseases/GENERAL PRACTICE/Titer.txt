
 Titer

 A titer is a measurement of the amount or concentration of a substance in a solution. It usually refers to the amount of  antibodies  found in a person's blood. Information 
 
 Blood titer measurements can help determine medical treatment. Antibody titers show if a person is immune to diseases such as  measles , chickenpox, or  hepatitis . They can also help measure harmful antibodies related to lupus.  A titer measurement is expressed as a ratio, such as 1:40. References
