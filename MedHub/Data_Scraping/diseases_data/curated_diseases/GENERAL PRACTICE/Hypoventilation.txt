
 Hypoventilation 
 
 
 
 To use the sharing features on this page, please enable JavaScript. 
 Hypoventilation is breathing that is too shallow or too slow to meet the needs of the body.  If a person hypoventilates, the body's carbon dioxide level rises. This causes a buildup of acid and too little oxygen in the blood. Symptoms A person with hypoventilation might feel sleepy. References
