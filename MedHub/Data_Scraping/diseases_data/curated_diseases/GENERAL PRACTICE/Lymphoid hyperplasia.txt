
 Lymphoid hyperplasia 
 
 
 
 To use the sharing features on this page, please enable JavaScript. 
 Lymphoid hyperplasia is an increase in the number of normal cells (called lymphocytes) that are contained in lymph nodes. This most often happens when there is an infection with bacteria, viruses, or other types of germs and is part of the body's reaction to the infection. Alternative Names
