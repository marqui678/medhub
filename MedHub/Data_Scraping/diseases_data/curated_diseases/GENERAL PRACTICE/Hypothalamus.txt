 The hypothalamus is an area of the brain that produces hormones that control: Body temperature Hunger Mood Release of hormones from many glands, especially the pituitary gland Sex drive Sleep Thirst Heart rate  Information 
 
 HYPOTHALAMIC DISEASE Hypothalamic dysfunction can occur as a result of diseases, including: Genetic causes (often present at birth or during childhood) Injury as a result of trauma, surgery or radiation Infection or inflammation  SYMPTOMS OF HYPOTHALAMIC DISEASE Because the hypothalamus controls so many different functions, hypothalamic disease can have many different symptoms, depending on the cause. The most common symptoms are: Increased appetite and rapid weight gain Extreme thirst and frequent urination ( diabetes insipidus ) Low body temperature Slow heart rate  References
